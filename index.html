unseen.

a letter from a man who's been quiet too long

To whoever actually stops and reads this… if anyone ever does —

There’s nothing in me worth admiring. I’ve known it for so long it feels like a scar that won’t heal. I wake up every day carrying this quiet shame, smiling at the world while something inside me slowly dies from being unseen for so many years.

I just want to be held. God, I want it so badly it hurts. Not some casual hug — I want arms that wrap around all of me, the heavy parts, the broken parts, the parts I hide from everyone. Someone patient enough to wait until I can scrape together a flight ticket so they can finally come here to Finland. Someone brave enough to look their parents in the eye and say they really want to meet me. Someone so in love with me they’d feel jealous if another girl even talked to me. I want to matter that much to someone.

But people rarely see past my appearance. That’s the truth that cuts deepest. In this world, looks are everything. Personality, character, maturity, loyalty, how hard I work, how deeply I feel — none of it seems to matter anymore. People care ten times more about how you look than who you actually are. I know appearance matters to some degree, but it shouldn’t matter so much that you completely ignore someone just because they aren’t some damn bodybuilder with perfect features. It shouldn’t make you invisible.

I’m not hideous. I’m just… ordinary. Average. And apparently that’s enough to be discarded. I watch people swipe past, glance and look away, judge in seconds. They never stay long enough to see that I’d treat them better than anyone ever has. That I’d be loyal as hell. That I’d actually listen. That I have a heart that still hopes even after all this silence.

So I stay quiet. I bottle it up. I carry the loneliness like it’s normal. Because admitting how desperately I want to be chosen — how badly I need someone to see me and still want me — feels more dangerous than staying alone. I’m tired of being the guy who’s “nice” but never enough. Tired of being overlooked while everyone chases the fantasy version of a man I’ll never be.

I still wonder if I’ll ever be enough for anyone. If there’s someone out there who will be patient, brave, and in love enough to cross the distance, to fight the doubts, to choose the real me over the perfect picture. Or if I’m doomed to stay unseen forever.

— Just a tired man in Finland, still hoping someone will see him
